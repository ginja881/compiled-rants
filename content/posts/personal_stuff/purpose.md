## Finding Purpose

Before I got into programming, I wanted to become a character artist.
Seeing how worlds were made from watching children's cartoons to playing games, I wanted to reciprocate the same feeling of wonder I 
had when I was much younger through making characers and bringing things to life from a canvas to screen.

To help with this goal, I enrolled myself in online artist classes going over fundamental topics, such as shading,
making still-life, drawing shapes, drawing lines, and basic anatomy.
At first, my aformentioned dream of creating worlds through art seemed visible. 
However, since I was diagnosed with a physical disability that impeded my fine-motor skills for drawing,
my passion/fire for art was slowy fading away because more complex tasks, such as gesture-poses and anatomy, became way more hard to perform. 
As a result, I gave up drawing because (even though I still tried my best and showed up) it didn't feel meaningful to me.



## Coming full Circle with Programming

Now, I've built a new world that gives me a new and similar feeling of creation that I found with art:
**A world of understanding computation from the source code of programming languages to silicon**

Ever since I took Harvard's CS50 computer science course, which went over the C language, basic computing, Python, and web development,
I was fascinated on how we can translate words into tangible execution. Hence, I decided to take the path of the systems programmer by
teaching myself relevant algorithms for low-level programming, computer architecture/elementary machine organization, and designing hardware/fault-tolerant systems. 

Now, after a short educational gap in my journey, I'm back and better than ever!! 
I want to continue building the world I envision alongside all of my friends and family that strongly support me!!!
